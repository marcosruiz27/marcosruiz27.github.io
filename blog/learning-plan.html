Learning Plan - Foundations

1 - What is your long term goal or career pathway?

I am currently working as an application support engineer in a Software Company, my goal is upskill and slowly start moving into a software developer/engineer role.

2 - A description of your strengths and limitations when it comes to learning.

I'm very commited when I feel motivated, I like to dedicate time and effort to projects I enjoy. I schedule time, organise what I want to achieve and stick to the plan. On the other hand, when I don't find something interesting or enjoyable, I sometimes procrastinate. It's harder to stick to the plan and keep motivated.

3 - What skills (non-technical/human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

Definetely collaboration. Even though I work well in groups I tend to work alone, that is something I would like to change. I'm looking forwarded to working in team projects and being able to interact with the rest of the cohort.

4 - A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme.

Except for this week and the week before that I had to travel for work, my weekdays schedules are always the same. So my plan is to dedicate 4 hours a day during weekdays (before or after work) and the necessary hours during weekends. Apart from work I don't have other obligations, I also included in the plan a morning run most days to clear my mind.

5 - A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way.

I will reach out for help in Discord to either Shrena or John whenever I feel stuck and can't make any progress in a period of 30 min or when I can anticipate that I won't be able to meet a deadline.

6 - A description of what you expect from the Facilitation team.

I hope they can provide guidance when I'm feeling stuck or encouragement when I feel frustrated.

7 - Any scheduling information such as block-out times when you are committed to other things. For example, you might have whānau commitments like school drop-offs. It's important to plan these in.

Nope. My brother is visiting in early December. I will probably spend two or three days with him if I see the course is going well and I'm finishing sprint activities on time.

Learning Plan - Bootcamp

What is your long term goal or career pathway?

I'm working in a Software Company as an application support engineer, my goal is upskill and slowly start moving into a software developer/engineer role. If possible I would like to stay in this company, but I don't rule out moving companies if the right opportunity comes.

What do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?

During foundations I had to find ways to deal with frustration. Taking a lot of short breaks worked well for me. My concern with Bootcamp is that time will be precious and I might not be able to take a lot of breaks so I might have to find other alternatives.

What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?

I'm not sure if should call it a challenge, but sometimes I'm not good at explaining what I want to say. I think what I'm saying makes sense, but it might not be easy to understand for other people. I guess I'm not very efficient when I structure ideas or explanations. My plan is to make sure to think before I express something (verbally or by writing) and make sure I'm providing enough information for the other person to understand me.

What non-technical skills - human skills - would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

Exactly the answer to the previous question. I'd like to get better at communicating and explaining ideas.

What are your expectations from the Bootcamp team?

I hope we can work well as a team, collaborate, support each other, share wins and cool tricks and maybe celebrate with some drinks when we finish the course.

What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp

I hope I can learn a lot, practice what I learn, enjoy the time it lasts. I hope I will finish without major issues. I want it to be challeinging, I want to enjoy experiecing those challenges. I hope I can get ideas for future projects.
