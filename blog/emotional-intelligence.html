Emotional Intelligence

What is IQ?

IQ stands for Intelligence Quotient. IQ measures cognitive abilities including reasoning, information processing and memory. IQ measurements demonstrate how you use information or logic skills to solve problems and predict outcomes. IQ tests also measure both long-term and short-term memory and assess how well you solve puzzles or recall past information. Some of the abilities related to IQ are:

  • Visual processing
  • Logical and quantitative reasoning
  • Short- and long-term memory
  • Word comprehension
  • General knowledge

What is EQ?

EQ stands for emotional quotient, or normally known as emotional intelligence. Emotional intelligence represents your ability to understand and manage your emotions and identify and relate to othersâ€™ emotions. Emotional intelligence describes general emotional awareness, enabling you to recognize specific emotions and address them appropriately. Social skills associated with EQ include collaboration, teamwork, conflict management and the ability to influence others. Some of the abilities related to EQ are:

  • Identifying emotions in themselves and others
  • Evaluating emotions in themselves and others
  • Controlling one's emotions
  • Expressing one's emotions
  • Using emotions in social situations
  • Relating others' emotions to one's own
